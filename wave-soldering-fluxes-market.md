<p><p>Wave soldering fluxes are essential in electronics manufacturing, categorized into corrosive and non-corrosive types. Corrosive fluxes contain chemicals that remove oxides and enhance solder adhesion but can damage components if not cleaned properly, making them suitable for quick production runs where reliability is not as critical. Non-corrosive fluxes, on the other hand, are designed to leave minimal residues and are safer for sensitive electronics, requiring more thorough cleaning but ensuring greater long-term reliability in assembled products.</p></p>
