<p><p>The Automotive Steering Angle Sensor market comprises two primary types: digital sensors and analog sensors. Digital sensors use electronic components to provide precise, real-time measurements of the steering angle, enhancing vehicle stability and control. They typically offer higher accuracy and better integration with advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS). In contrast, analog sensors produce continuous voltage signals proportional to the steering angle, offering simplicity and cost-effectiveness. Both types play crucial roles in modern vehicles, contributing to safety and driving performance.</p></p>
