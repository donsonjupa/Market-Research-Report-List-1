<p><p>The infant phototherapy devices market encompasses various types of light sources used for treating neonatal jaundice. Fluorescent lamps (FL) are traditional, offering broad-spectrum light but with heat emission. Light-emitting diodes (LEDs) are energy-efficient, have longer lifespans, and produce less heat, making them a popular choice. Quartz halogen lamps provide intense light and rapid heating but are less common due to safety concerns. Gas discharge tubes are also utilized for their effectiveness, yet they often have a larger footprint and less flexibility.</p></p>
