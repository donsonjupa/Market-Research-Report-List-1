<p><p>The Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA) drugs market comprises two main types: prescription and over-the-counter (OTC) drugs. Prescription drugs typically include disease-modifying antirheumatic drugs (DMARDs), biologics, and corticosteroids, which require a doctor's supervision for managing RA symptoms and preventing joint damage. In contrast, OTC drugs encompass non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) and analgesics, available without a prescription for symptomatic relief. The distinction is crucial, as prescription medications often address underlying disease progression, while OTC options primarily manage pain and inflammation.</p></p>
