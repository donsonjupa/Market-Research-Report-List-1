<p><p>Notch filters are essential in signal processing, specifically designed to eliminate unwanted frequencies while allowing others to pass. The market is divided into two types: tunable and non-tunable. Tunable notch filters offer adjustable frequency rejection, catering to various applications and allowing users to customize performance as needed. In contrast, non-tunable notch filters are fixed in frequency, providing reliability and simplicity for specific applications. Both types serve critical roles in telecommunications, audio processing, and instrumentation by ensuring signal clarity.</p></p>
