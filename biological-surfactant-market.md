<p><p>Biological surfactants, also known as biosurfactants, are surface-active substances produced by living microorganisms. Consumers are increasingly favoring biodegradable options, prompting manufacturers to explore biosurfactant applications that align with eco-friendly practices. The agriculture sector is witnessing substantial growth, as biological surfactants enhance soil properties and aid in the effective delivery of agrochemicals. </p><p>In the personal care and cosmetics industry, the shift towards natural formulations is fueling demand for biosurfactants, which provide gentle cleansing and moisturizing effects. Additionally, advancements in biotechnology are leading to the discovery of new microbial strains and production methods, further propelling market growth. The combination of regulatory support and rising consumer trends toward sustainability positions the biological surfactant market for robust expansion in the coming years.</p></p>
