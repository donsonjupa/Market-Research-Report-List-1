<p><p>The High-Reliability Ceramic Substrate market predominantly features two types: HTCC (High-Temperature Co-fired Ceramic) and LTCC (Low-Temperature Co-fired Ceramic) substrates. HTCC substrates are designed for high-temperature applications, providing excellent thermal stability and electrical insulation, making them suitable for demanding environments. In contrast, LTCC substrates are co-fired at lower temperatures, enabling integration of passive components and finer features, ideal for complex and compact electronic designs. Both types cater to industries requiring exceptional reliability and performance, such as aerospace, automotive, and telecommunications.</p></p>
