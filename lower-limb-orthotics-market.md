<p><p>The lower limb orthotics market is primarily categorized into dynamic and static orthotics. Dynamic orthotics are designed to support movement and provide flexibility, often used for conditions requiring active rehabilitation, such as after an injury or surgery. In contrast, static orthotics are more rigid and aim to immobilize or stabilize the limb, commonly used for managing structural deformities or providing support during recovery. Both types play crucial roles in improving mobility and enhancing overall quality of life for patients.</p></p>
