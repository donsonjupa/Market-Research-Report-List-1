<p><p>In-Memory Databases are categorized into two primary market types: Online Analytical Processing (OLAP) and Online Transaction Processing (OLTP). OLAP databases focus on complex queries and data analysis, enabling rapid reporting and decision-making by storing data in a manner optimized for read-heavy operations. In contrast, OLTP databases are designed for high-transaction environments, ensuring fast processing of numerous short transactions and maintaining data integrity. Both types leverage in-memory processing for enhanced speed and performance across diverse applications.</p></p>
